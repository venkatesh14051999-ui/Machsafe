The Problem

Building Safety Act compliance
is broken for most APs

Since April 2024, every Accountable Person managing a higher-risk building faces personal criminal liability. Most have no system to meet their obligations.

ISSUE 01

No Golden Thread system

Most APs store safety documents in email folders or SharePoint. No version control, no gap detection, no regulatory structure — completely indefensible under the Building Safety Act 2022.

⚠ Regulatory exposure
ISSUE 02

Compliance gaps are invisible

Identifying missing or inadequate documents requires expert knowledge of six overlapping regulations. Without specialist help, APs don't know what they're missing until the BSR asks.

£3,000–£8,000 per audit
ISSUE 03

Safety Case costs a fortune

A compliant Safety Case report requires a qualified fire engineer. It takes days to produce and costs thousands — meaning most APs can't afford to keep it current.

£2,000–£8,000 per report
ISSUE 04

BAC requests arrive with no warning

The BSR is actively issuing Building Assessment Certificate requests to APs. Without a ready Safety Case and complete evidence package, APs face enforcement action.

Enforcement + prosecution risk
⚖️

Building Safety Act 2022 — Personal Criminal Liability

Accountable Persons face unlimited financial penalties and personal prosecution for failure to maintain the Golden Thread, produce a Safety Case, or respond to BSR Building Assessment Certificate requests. Compliance is not optional.
